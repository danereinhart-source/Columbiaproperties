If there is one habit that saves Arizona landlords more money and stress than any other, it is documenting the condition of the property thoroughly at move-in and move-out. The vast majority of security deposit disputes come down to a single question: can you prove the condition changed during the tenancy? Good inspections answer that question before it is ever asked.

Why Inspections Matter So Much

Arizona law treats the security deposit as the tenant's money, and you can only withhold for damage beyond normal wear and tear. The burden is effectively on you to justify any deduction. Without dated evidence of the home's condition at move-in, a deduction at move-out becomes your word against the tenant's, a contest you are likely to lose if it reaches a courtroom.

The core principle: A deduction is only as strong as your move-in documentation. You cannot prove a tenant damaged something if you can't show its condition when they moved in.

The Move-In Inspection

The move-in inspection sets your baseline. Done well, it protects both you and the tenant. Here is how to approach it:

Use a Written Checklist

Go room by room and document the condition of walls, flooring, ceilings, windows, doors, fixtures, appliances, countertops, cabinets, and outdoor areas. Note existing wear, marks, or defects honestly. A detailed, room-by-room form is far more useful than a vague "good condition" note.

Photograph and Date Everything

Take clear, dated photos (and video) of every room and every existing flaw. Capture appliance model and serial numbers, the condition of carpet and paint, and the state of the yard, pool, and HVAC. More documentation is always better; storage is free, disputes are not.

Have the Tenant Sign

Walk through with the tenant when possible, give them a chance to note anything you missed, and have both parties sign and date the report. Provide the tenant a copy. A signed, mutually agreed baseline is your strongest possible evidence.

Inspections During the Tenancy

You do not have to wait until move-out to check on the home. Periodic inspections during the lease, with proper advance notice to the tenant as required by Arizona law, let you catch maintenance issues early, confirm the home is being cared for, and spot unauthorized occupants or pets. In Arizona, a mid-lease check before monsoon season or summer can prevent expensive surprises. Always give the legally required notice and respect the tenant's right to quiet enjoyment.

The Move-Out Inspection

When the tenancy ends, the move-out inspection is where your earlier diligence pays off:

  • Inspect promptly after the tenant returns the keys.
  • Use the same checklist from move-in so you are comparing like for like, room by room.
  • Photograph everything again with dates, mirroring your move-in shots for easy side-by-side comparison.
  • Distinguish damage from normal wear: faded paint, minor scuffs, and lightly worn carpet are normal wear; large holes, significant stains, and broken fixtures are damage.

Tying It Back to the Deposit

Remember Arizona's rules: you must return the deposit, or an itemized statement of deductions with any remaining balance, within 14 days of the tenant vacating and returning the keys. Your inspection photos, checklist, and repair receipts are the documentation that makes any deduction defensible. Be reasonable and specific; inflated or vague charges are what invite disputes and damage awards.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the move-in inspection because the tenant seems trustworthy
  • Taking only a handful of photos instead of comprehensive documentation
  • Failing to date records or get the tenant's signature
  • Charging tenants for normal wear and tear, which is not permitted
  • Missing the 14-day deposit deadline
